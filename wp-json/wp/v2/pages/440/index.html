The Open Science Framework (OSF)<\/a> is a free and open source web application built by the Center for Open Science<\/a> that provides all researchers with a mechanism to organize, collaborate, share, and store components of their research projects. Features of the OSF include:<\/p>\n

    \n
  • Individual user dashboards<\/a> to manage multiple projects, both private and public<\/li>\n
  • Collaborative capabilities<\/a> to share data within and beyond institutions and research groups<\/li>\n
  • Version control<\/a> measures<\/li>\n
  • Access control<\/a> at both project and file levels<\/li>\n
  • Persistent identifiers (DOIs)<\/a> for all work and a platform to register and share work, whether in process or complete<\/li>\n
  • Storage add-ons<\/a> for bringing together documents, data, code, and related products that are stored in different spaces such as Github, Google Drive, and Dropbox<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    OSF is designed to function with your workflow and to facilitate sharing at any point in the research process, either privately among your peers, or publicly with a persistent identifier and a citation.<\/p>\n

    OSF for MIT<\/h2>\n

    MIT affiliates can now sign into OSF with your MIT credentials here<\/a>\u00a0(Choose “login” and then select “login through your institution”). Use this OSF for MIT portal<\/a>\u00a0to affiliate your public projects with MIT, view public projects with which others at MIT are involved, and make serendipitous connections for future collaborations.<\/p>\n
