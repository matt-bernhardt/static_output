Repositories can help you:<\/p>\n

    \n
  • manage your data<\/li>\n
  • cite your data by supplying a persistent identifier<\/li>\n
  • facilitate discovery of your data<\/li>\n
  • preserve your data over time<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    Selecting your repository<\/h2>\n

    In choosing a repository for your data, first consider:<\/p>\n

      \n
    • Does your funder or a publisher require or suggest a specific repository?
      \nSee our Research Funder Requirements<\/a> page for information on some of the common funders.<\/li>\n
    • Is there a repository specific for your discipline or type of data?
      \nThe       Registry of Research Data Repositories<\/a> can be helpful in browsing options.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

      While there are many options to choose from, we\u2019ve highlighted a small set of trusted repositories \u2013 Harvard Dataverse<\/a>, Zenodo<\/a>, and Dryad<\/a> \u2013 to help researchers quickly review key features in making their decisions.<\/p>\n

      Please refer to each repository\u2019s documentation for the most current information. If you see an error in the information below or would like to discuss what might work for you and your data, contact data-management@mit.edu<\/a>.<\/p>\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n
