A data management plan (DMP) will help you manage your data, meet funder requirements, and help others use your data if shared.<\/p>\n

<\/a>The DMPTool<\/a>\u00a0is a web-based tool that helps you construct data management plans using templates that address specific funder requirements. From within this tool, you can save your plans, access MIT-specific information & resources, and request a review of your DMP by a member of our Data Management Services team.\u00a0To access the MIT-customized DMPTool, choose “Massachusetts Institute of Technology” as your institution to log in via Touchstone.<\/p>\n

Alternatively, you can use the questions below and any specific data management requirements<\/a> from your funding agency to write your data management plan. Additional resources for creating plans<\/a>\u00a0are also provided below.<\/p>\n

    \n
  1. \u00a0Project, experiment, and data description\n
      \n
    • What\u2019s the purpose of the research?<\/li>\n
    • What is the data? How and in what format will the data be collected? Is it numerical data, image data, text sequences, or modeling data?<\/li>\n
    • How much data will be generated for this research?<\/li>\n
    • How long will the data be collected and how often will it change?<\/li>\n
    • Are you using data that someone else produced? If so, where is it from?<\/li>\n
    • Who is responsible for managing the data? Who will ensure that the data management plan is carried out?<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n<\/li>\n
    • Documentation, organization, and storage\n
        \n
      • What documentation<\/a> will you be creating in order to make the data understandable by other researchers?<\/li>\n
      • Are you using metadata that is standard to your field? How will the metadata be managed and stored?<\/li>\n
      • What file formats<\/a> will be used? Do these formats conform to an open standard and\/or are they proprietary?<\/li>\n
      • Are you using a file format that is standard to your field? If not, how will you document the alternative you are using?<\/li>\n
      • What directory and file naming<\/a> convention will be used?<\/li>\n
      • What are your local storage and backup procedures<\/a>? Will this data require secure storage?<\/li>\n
      • What tools or software are required to read or view the data?<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n<\/li>\n
      • Access, sharing, and re-use\n
          \n
        • Who has the right to manage this data? Is it the responsibility of the PI, student, lab, MIT, or funding agency?<\/li>\n
        • What data will be shared<\/a>, when, and how?<\/li>\n
        • Does sharing the data raise privacy, ethical, or confidentiality concerns<\/a>? \u00a0Do you have a plan to protect or anonymize data, if needed?<\/li>\n
        • Who holds intellectual property rights for the data and other information created by the project? Will any copyrighted or licensed material be used? Do you have permission to use\/disseminate this material?<\/li>\n
        • Are there any patent- or technology-licensing-related restrictions on data sharing associated with this grant? The Technology Licensing Office (TLO)<\/a> can provide this information.<\/li>\n
        • Will this research be published in a journal<\/a> that requires the underlying data to accompany articles?<\/li>\n
        • Will there be any embargoes on the data?<\/li>\n
        • Will you permit re-use<\/a>, redistribution, or the creation of new tools, services, data sets, or products (derivatives)? Will commercial use be allowed?<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n<\/li>\n
        • Archiving\n
            \n
          • How will you be archiving the data? Will you be storing it in an archive or repository for long-term access? If not, how will you preserve access to the data?<\/li>\n
          • Is a discipline-specific repository<\/a> available? If not, you could consider depositing your data into DSpace@MIT<\/a>. Email us at data-management@mit.edu if you\u2019re interested in using DSpace@MIT to store your data.<\/li>\n
          • How will you prepare data for preservation or data sharing? Will the data need to be anonymized or converted to more stable file formats?<\/li>\n
          • Are software or tools needed to use the data? Will these be archived?<\/li>\n
          • How long should the data be retained? 3-5 years, 10 years, or forever?<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n<\/li>\n<\/ol>\n
