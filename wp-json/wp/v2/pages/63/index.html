Confidentiality<\/h2>\n
    \n
  • MIT regulations: MIT researchers conducting human subject research should consult the MIT Committee on the Use of Humans as Experimental Subjects (COUHES)<\/a> in planning for data management and sharing.<\/li>\n
  • Health research regulations: Researchers need to adhere to privacy law regarding personal health information. See the HIPPA Privacy Rule, Information for Researchers<\/a>.<\/li>\n
  • Informed consent: Researchers should include a provision for data sharing. See the guides to informed consent from the U.K. Data Archive<\/a> and ICPSR<\/a>.<\/li>\n
  • Maintaining confidentiality: Data made publicly available should not contain information that could risk the confidentiality of their participants.\n

        Intellectual Property<\/h2>\n

        Sharing data that you have produced or collected yourself:<\/p>\n

          \n
        • Data is not copyrightable. Particular expressions of data, such as a table in a book, can be copyrightable.<\/li>\n
        • Promote sharing and unlimited use of your data by making it available under an Open Data Commons<\/a> or\u00a0Creative Commons license<\/a>.<\/li>\n
        • If you are uncertain as to your rights to disseminate data, contact us<\/a> for a consultation.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

          Sharing data that you have collected from other sources:<\/p>\n

            \n
          • Licensed data can have restrictions in the way it can be used or shared downstream.<\/li>\n
          • Many databases licensed by the MIT LIbraries allow for educational and research use, and sharing limited portions of data.\u00a0 For questions on terms of use for databases licensed by the MIT Libraries, email scholarlypub@mit.edu<\/a>.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
